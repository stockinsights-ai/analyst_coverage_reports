Financial Projections for Q2 FY25: Mahindra & Mahindra Ltd

1. Basis for Projections

The projections for Q2 FY25 are based on:

  • Historical financial performance up to Q1 FY25.
  • Management's guidance from the latest earnings call, indicating a mid to high teens growth for FY25.
  • Recent announcements and market developments impacting Mahindra & Mahindra Ltd.
  • Current industry trends and economic outlook.

2. Assumptions for Q2 FY25 Projections

  • Revenue Growth: A projected QoQ revenue growth of 5%, considering seasonal factors and management's optimistic outlook.
  • EBITDA Margin: Maintained at the Q1 FY25 level of approximately 19.46% of revenue.
  • Other Income: Slight decrease due to trends, estimated at INR 720 crores for Q2 FY25.
  • Depreciation: Assumed to marginally increase to INR 1,260 crores, reflecting ongoing capital expenditure.
  • Interest Expense: Projected to rise by 5% QoQ to INR 2,213.07 crores due to increased borrowings.
  • Tax Rate: Estimated at an average effective tax rate of 23.5% based on recent quarters.

4. Assumptions Used in Forecasting and Underlying Risks Involved

4.1 Key Assumptions

  • Steady Market Demand: Assumes continued demand in the automotive and farm equipment sectors.
  • Cost Management: Operational efficiencies and cost-saving initiatives will maintain EBITDA margins.
  • Stable Commodity Prices: Raw material costs remain stable without significant inflationary pressures.
  • No Major Supply Chain Disruptions: Assumes a smooth supply chain, particularly for semiconductor components.

4.2 Underlying Risks

  • Market Volatility: Economic uncertainties or changes in consumer demand could impact sales.
  • Regulatory Changes: New regulations or compliance requirements may increase operating costs.
  • Commodity Price Fluctuations: Increases in raw material prices could compress margins.
  • Supply Chain Constraints: Global shortages, especially in semiconductors, could affect production schedules.
  • Interest Rate Risks: Rising interest rates may increase financing costs and impact net profitability.

5. Conclusion

Based on the above assumptions and considering management's positive outlook, Mahindra & Mahindra Ltd is projected to show moderate growth in Q2 FY25. The company is expected to maintain its profitability margins while navigating potential risks in the operating environment.
